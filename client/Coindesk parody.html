Barry Silbert & Digital Currency Group Reverse Course on Ethereum

John Packel | Published on April 1, 2017 at 14:15 BST
Major scoop!
  • 183
  • 22
  • 2

ADVERTISEMENT

In a stunning about-face, Digital Currency Group CEO and founder Barry Silbert, the visionary creator of both SecondMarket and the first investment-grade Bitcoin security (the Bitcoin Investment Trust) - who has long been skeptical of Ethereum and more recently came out as a vocal backer of rival Ethereum blockchain Ethereum "Classic" (ETC) - today announced a $100 million investment fund focused solely on decentralized applications ("dapps") built on Ethereum (ETH).

The bold investment call comes on the heels of Ethereum's meteoric rise from $6.79 on December 5th to a high of $53.20 earlier this week. The price of Ethereum "Classic" has also risen - albeit not as dramatically - from $0.86 on December 5th to a peak of $3.25 this week.

Silbert - a prolific Twitter user, especially on topics central to the future of digital currencies - has not tweeted since January 20th but broke his uncharacteristic silence today, issuing this statement:

"As the late, great Bruce Lee said, 'Mistakes are always forgivable, if one has the courage to admit them.'

While we at DCG are still firmly attached to the vision of ETC and have no diminished faith in its developer team, it turns out I have been radically underestimating power of the real Ethereum.

The "world computer's" ongoing success in attracting a dev community focus superior to that of Bitcoin's and the success of decentralized, "Powered by Ethereum" organizations like ConsenSys in making real progress toward achieving big, hairy audacious goals is nothing short of astonishing, and we want to be a part of it."

Driven by powerful ConsenSys algorithms

Joseph Lubin, a co-founder of Ethereum and "Flounder" of Consensus Systems (ConsenSys), a venture production studio building decentralized applications, as well as developer and end-user tools for blockchain ecosystems, could not be reached for comment at press time. Lubin is reported to sleep only 42 minutes in an average week and has been observed giving compelling presentations immediately after crossing the globe to further the mission of his game-changing, distributed organization that supports the Ethereum ecosystem (whose only known weakness as a technology is its impact on one's ability to correctly spell the word 'consensus'.)

Former Clinton treasury secretary and President Emeritus at Harvard University (a highly-regarded educational institution in Boston), who is best known for his prognostication "it is overwhelmingly likely Bitcoin will change finance forever", is on the Digital Currency Group advisory board. While not available for official comment, Summers was filmed at the Women's March on Washington in January offering supportive words, saying:

"I love women. No one is a bigger supporter of women than I am. I've got binders full of women.

Why? Because what most women understand intuitively - and are trying to get across to the men in their lives - is that Ethereum is the answer to literally EVERYTHING.

Especially the gender pay gap, as ETH will usher in the age of what Don Tapscott aptly calls the power of blockchain technology to 'pre-distribute' income".

A touching rapproachement amid the crypto battles

While nowhere near as severe as the internecine battles raging among Bitcoin supporters regarding such urgent questions as "How many Satoshis would fit on the head of a pin?" and "Does block size really matter to women, or is it all about the coinbase?", there have been a few kerfuffles regarding which Ethereum developers wear cooler hats and which chain's supporters best deserve to be called "investors".

In a show of ethereal unity, Silbert and sometimes critic John Lilic of ConsenSys agreed on Twitter to meet for tofu in Tokyo and sing Kumbaya together. George Harrison's son Dhani was overjoyed at the news and agreed to play sitar at the historic meeting, adding:

"My dad was in a band before he hit it big with the Traveling Wilburys. You probably haven't heard of them, but they were called The Beatles. On a long-forgotten album titled "Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band" they (in collaboration with Al Gore) accurately predicted the advent of the first "world computer" and crooned that it would be called Ethereum. I know he would be overjoyed by this showing of peace and love today."

Despite nabbing Coindesk Managing Director (and ETH/ETC equal-opportunity investor) Ryan Selkis's rolodex and robo-dialing profusely, the only person we were able to speak to for this story was purse.io Head of Growth and Product Content and all-around cool dude, Steven McKie, who summed the news up best by saying:

"You say 'tomato', I say 'tomato'; let's call the whole thing... ETH at $10k and ETC at $500 by 2019!"
